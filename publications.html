Selected Papers

What drives the loss and gain of salt marsh in recent decades? (in preparation). Lin, H., Fagherazzi, S., Fichot, C. G.

This forthcoming study integrates global salt‑marsh change records with tidal range and wave climate data to identify the drivers of marsh expansion and decline and to propose a resilience/vulnerability framework for future management.

Survivorship bias of optical remote sensing of suspended sediment concentration (submitted). Lin, H., Fichot, C. G., Yu, Q., Wang, Y., Wang, A., Gao, S.

We confront a fundamental limitation in satellite observations of the coastal ocean: they preferentially see “fair weather.” Because optical sensors cannot penetrate clouds, they systematically miss high-cloud periods—intervals that often coincide with distinct dynamical regimes—which skews our understanding of global coastal sediment dynamics

Estimating tidally sediment flux in salt marshes using phase folding based on sporadic and asynchronous datasets (under review). Lin, H., Gonzalez, J. R. P., Fagherazzi, S., Fichot, C. G.

This manuscript uses a phase‑folding technique to infer tidally integrated net sediment flux from irregular and asynchronous in‑situ and satellite observations, enabling robust budget estimates for salt‑marsh systems.

Assessing potential to quantify multi‑period SSC variations using satellites of different temporal resolution (Science of the Total Environment, 2022). Lin, H., Yu, Q., Wang, Y., Gao, S.

This study evaluates how satellites with varying revisit times can capture multi‑period suspended‑sediment concentration variations and provides guidance for selecting remote‑sensing platforms for coastal sediment studies.

Identification, extraction and interpretation of multi‑period SSC variations from unevenly spaced observations (Marine Geology, 2022). Lin, H., Yu, Q., Wang, Y., Gao, S.

By analysing unevenly spaced observations, this article develops techniques to identify and interpret multi‑period suspended‑sediment variations, offering insights into sediment transport mechanisms.

Geomorphology and sediment dynamics of the Liyashan oyster reefs, Jiangsu Coast, China (Acta Oceanologica Sinica, 2021). Lin, H., Yu, Q., Du, Z., Fan, Y., Wang, Y., Gao, S.

This paper combines field surveys, numerical modelling and satellite imagery to investigate the morphology and sedimentary processes of the Liyashan oyster reefs, revealing drivers of reef evolution and degradation.

About Me

Hello! I am Hangjie Lin, a coastal geoscientist currently pursuing my Ph.D. in the Department of Earth & Environment at Boston University. Prior to joining BU, I earned my M.S. and B.S. from Nanjing University, where I studied marine geoscience. My research seeks to understand how coastal landscapes respond to natural variability and human influences.

I combine field observations, numerical modelling and remote sensing to quantify sediment fluxes in salt marshes, investigate the forced evolution of tidal flats and track turbidity variations across multiple time scales. Through these studies I hope to improve coastal resilience under climate change and inform sustainable management of intertidal ecosystems.
